**Short bio:** <br/> 
I am a second-year Ph.D. student in Engineering at the University of Tokyo where 
I also obtained my Master’s degree. I hold a double Bachelor’s in Control Theory and Automation Engineering from the University of Bologna and Tongji University (Shanghai, China).  
I am originally from <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cJj57iXlIqw">Bologna, Italy</a>, but I have had been spending the last ~20% of my current lifespan in Asia (one+ year in Shanghai, several more in Tokyo, Japan).

**Research:** <br/> 
My work focuses on the symbiosis between deep learning, dynamical systems and numerical methods. I am committed in ensuring the succeses achieved by deep learning methods to other engineering domains, such as robotics, control systems and biology.

To achieve this vision, my research follows <b>two fundamental principles</b>:

<ul>
  <li>High-quality ML software standards (including model heuristics and massively parallel training) should be exported and adapted to the requirements of other engineering areas;</li>
  <li>Domain expertise in the form of mathematical modeling and advanced numerical methods should be embedded in the learning model whenever possible, in order to guide other design decisions and ensure a satisfactory baseline performance.</li>
</ul>

I am a core maintainer and contributor to various open-source libraries for neural differential equations and numerical methods for neural networks.
